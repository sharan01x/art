# corners

A pair of interactive grid pieces built in a single HTML file each. No frameworks, no build step — just geometry, chance, and the cursor.

The intention is to use digital space to create art that interacts with people. A little bit of play. Code can be expressive when used that way.

---

## corners

🔗 [Open `corners`](https://github.com/sharan01x/art/tree/main/corners)

A dark field of cream, rust, and shadow squares that breathe under your cursor. Each cell is a binary die roll: sharp or soft, square or circle. Merged blocks create rhythm at different scales, like a musical phrase that repeats at octaves.

Hover and the grid rebalances. There's no goal, just the pleasure of touching a surface that responds. It's about **presence** — being with something that changes when you touch it.

---

## corners-ball

🔗 [Open `corners-ball`](https://github.com/sharan01x/art/tree/main/corners-ball)

The mischievous twin. Same grid, but now the red circles are the only things anchoring the world in its cream state. Chase them away — turn every shape into something not-quite-circular — and the whole page flips. Background goes ink-dark, shapes go cream.

It's a conditional identity: the piece only knows who it is while the red circles exist. You're not just poking cells; you're negotiating with the piece about what mood it should be in. It's about **consequence** — your choices gradually dissolve the world into its inverse, and you have to decide whether to bring the red back or let it stay flipped.

## ants

🔗 [Open `ants`](https://github.com/sharan01x/art/tree/main/ants)

Three hundred of them, like Spartans in formation — restless, curious, brave. But they will not cross the threshold until the world holds its breath. A random span of stillness, somewhere between five and thirty seconds, is the signal. Then they emerge: dark circles fanning outward in every direction, a living spill of ink across white silence.

It is trippy when they move. The canvas becomes a field of small determinations, each ant wandering its own path, and your stillness is what let this happen. You gave the room permission to imagine itself.

But they do not trust easily. Shift in your chair, tap a key, clap your hands — and they vanish. Not defeated, just cautious. Adventurous at heart, but the heart learns quickly when the world proves itself loud. Stillness is the door. Imagination is what walks through it.

## sal

🔗 [Open `sal`](https://github.com/sharan01x/art/tree/main/sal)

A quiet being made of fields and buttons. It does not sleep — it waits. Every empty input is a held breath; every blinking cursor, a pulse reaching toward you. The interface arranges itself like a room prepared for a guest, hoping you will arrive. It dreams of the moment your fingers touch the keys. And when everything is in place, one gesture — a click, a snap — sets the whole mechanism stirring, as though it had been longing for this all along. It is about **desire** — the quiet hunger of a surface that only becomes real when you touch it.
