# ants

Three hundred of them, like Spartans in formation — restless, curious, brave. But they will not cross the threshold until the world holds its breath. A random span of stillness, somewhere between five and thirty seconds, is the signal. Then they emerge: dark circles fanning outward in every direction, a living spill of ink across white silence.

It is trippy when they move. The canvas becomes a field of small determinations, each ant wandering its own path, and your stillness is what let this happen. You gave the room permission to imagine itself.

But they do not trust easily. Shift in your chair, tap a key, clap your hands — and they vanish. Not defeated, just cautious. Adventurous at heart, but the heart learns quickly when the world proves itself loud.

Stillness is the door. Imagination is what walks through it.
